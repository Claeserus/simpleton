Consistency over Intensity

Why 15 minutes a day beats a 10-hour marathon.

The Myth of the Weekend Warrior

Many people think that learning to code requires locking yourself in a room for an entire weekend and emerging as a developer. In reality, this is the fastest way to burn out and forget everything you learned.

The Science of "Spacing"

Your brain needs time to build new neural pathways. When you sleep, your brain "replays" the logic you practiced during the day. By coding just 15 to 30 minutes every single day, you give your brain 7 different opportunities per week to cement that knowledge.

"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit."

If you get stuck on a CSS layout or a broken link, don't let it ruin your day. Spend 15 minutes trying to fix it, and if it's still broken, walk away. You'll be surprised how often the answer "clicks" while you're brushing your teeth the next morning.
