Coding is Just Logic in Disguise

The biggest wall beginners hit isn't the difficulty of the language; it's the fear of the unknown. We see lines of code and think it's a foreign language reserved for geniuses.

The Sandwich Algorithm

Imagine explaining how to make a peanut butter sandwich to someone who has never seen one. You have to be precise:
1. Pick up the knife.
2. Open the jar.
3. Spread the butter.

That is all coding is—breaking a big goal into tiny, logical steps that a computer (which is actually quite "simple") can follow.
